⚖️ Legal & Compliance

Protecting AZ Learner and staying on the right side of the law

Legal Foundation Protects Everything You Build

Operating a digital platform in Ghana comes with legal obligations. Getting these right early saves you from costly problems later — and makes AZ Learner credible to schools, sponsors and institutional partners.

1. Register Your Business in Ghana

Operating as an unregistered business creates risk and limits your ability to open bank accounts, sign contracts, or receive grants.

  • Register with the Registrar General's Department (RGD) at rgd.gov.gh — this is Ghana's official business registration body
  • Choose an appropriate structure: Sole Proprietorship (simplest), Partnership, or Limited Liability Company (most protection)
  • Obtain a Ghana Revenue Authority (GRA) Tax Identification Number (TIN) — required for most formal contracts
  • Open a dedicated business bank account — never mix personal and business finances
  • The full registration process typically costs under GH₵500 and takes 5–10 business days

💡 Quick Win: Register as a sole proprietorship to start — it's the fastest and cheapest. You can convert to a limited liability company later when you have revenue and a team. Don't let paperwork delay your launch.

2. Data Protection & Student Privacy

You collect personal data from students — names, emails, academic records, and payment details. Ghana's Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843) requires you to handle this responsibly.

  • Register with the Data Protection Commission (DPC) if you process personal data — dataprotection.org.gh
  • Only collect data you actually need — don't ask for more information than is necessary
  • Never sell student data to third parties — this destroys trust and violates the law
  • Store all user data securely — use encrypted databases and enforce strong password policies
  • Give students the ability to request deletion of their account and data

3. Terms of Service & Privacy Policy

These two documents are legally essential. They define the relationship between AZ Learner and its users and protect you if disputes arise.

  • Your Terms of Service must cover: acceptable use, content ownership, payment terms, refund policy, account termination
  • Your Privacy Policy must explain: what data you collect, how it's used, how it's stored, and how users can request deletion
  • Display both documents clearly on your platform — require new users to accept them at sign-up
  • Review and update them every 12 months or whenever your data practices change
  • Use plain language — legal jargon that students can't understand defeats the purpose

💡 Free Resource: Ghana's Data Protection Commission offers guidance documents for small businesses. Google's legal document templates are also a good starting point — customise them for your context and have a lawyer review the final version.

4. Intellectual Property

Your platform name, logo, course content, and code are intellectual property. Protect them before someone else claims them.

  • Trademark "AZ Learner" with the Trademarks Registry at the Registrar General's Department — this prevents others from using your brand name
  • Register your domain name and any variations — azlearner.com, azlearner.gh, azlearner.net
  • Ensure all content created by tutors or contractors is covered by a written agreement transferring intellectual property rights to AZ Learner
  • Never use copyrighted textbook content, past question papers, or images without permission or licence

5. Payment & Financial Compliance

Processing student payments comes with its own set of obligations in Ghana.

  • Use licensed payment processors — Paystack, Hubtel, and ExpressPay are all regulated for Ghana
  • Issue receipts for all transactions — this is a legal requirement and builds student trust
  • Register for VAT with the GRA once your annual revenue exceeds GH₵200,000
  • Keep accurate financial records for at least 7 years — this is required by Ghanaian tax law
  • File annual tax returns with the GRA, even if you have minimal revenue in the early years

6. Working with Minors

If AZ Learner serves students under 18, you have additional legal and ethical obligations.

  • Obtain parental or guardian consent before collecting data from users under 16
  • Do not allow direct payment by minors without parental consent
  • Ensure all content is appropriate for the age range of your users
  • Implement basic safeguarding policies — especially for any live sessions or community features

⚠️ Legal Risk: Operating without registering your business or complying with data protection law exposes you to fines and enforcement action. A single complaint from a parent or partner about data misuse can damage your reputation permanently. Compliance is not optional — it's the foundation of trust.
