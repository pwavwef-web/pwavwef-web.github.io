Communication Style Guide

Master the AZ Learner tone of voice. Every message you send as an ambassador represents the platform — make it count.

Tone of Voice

Communication is 7% words, 38% tone, and 55% body language. As an ambassador, how you say something matters as much as what you say.

Friendly & Approachable

Speak like a peer, not a salesperson. Be the friend who's excited to share something useful.

Encouraging

Focus on what students can achieve. Lead with benefits, not features.

Clear & Concise

Avoid jargon. Use plain everyday language — if a secondary school student can understand it, it's good.

Honest

Never overpromise. Stick to features that are actually live on the platform right now.

Messaging Dos & Don'ts

DO

  • Lead with the student benefit: "You can find past questions in seconds"
  • Use first-person stories: "This feature helped me prepare for my finals"
  • Ask open questions: "How are you preparing for exams?"
  • Always include a clear call-to-action with your referral link
  • Proofread all written communications before sending

DON'T

  • Compare AZ Learner to competitors by name
  • Promise features that are not yet live on the platform
  • Use ALL CAPS in social media posts for emphasis
  • Share students' personal data or private conversations publicly
  • Post anything that could embarrass AZ Learner or fellow students

Active Listening in Conversations

Great ambassadors listen more than they speak. Use these techniques in every student interaction:

①

Paraphrase — Repeat back what you heard: "So you're saying you struggle with keeping up with the workload?"

②

Ask open-ended questions — "Can you tell me more about how you currently study?"

③

Avoid interrupting — Let the student finish their thought before responding.

④

Summarise at the end — "It sounds like you'd benefit most from the study group feature — let me show you."
