Study Group Facilitation Guide

Step-by-step structure, icebreakers, discussion prompts and techniques for handling group dynamics. Run sessions your peers will come back to.

Before the Session

Choose a focused topic — one subject or one exam topic per session. Broader sessions lose focus and effectiveness.

Keep groups small — Invite 4–8 students. Larger groups lose engagement and intimacy.

Send a prep note 24 hours before — Include: topic, time, location, and one thing to read/review in advance.

Set up the AZ Learner session — Use the platform's study circle feature if running the session virtually.

Session Structure (60 Minutes)

0:00 – 0:05

5 min

Welcome & Icebreaker

"What's one thing you're unsure about on this topic?" — Low stakes, builds psychological safety immediately.

0:05 – 0:15

10 min

Quick Knowledge Check

2–3 rapid-fire questions on the topic. This reveals gaps and sets the focus for the session. Use AZ Learner past questions if available.

0:15 – 0:40

25 min

Core Content — Rotate Presenters

Each student presents one section. Teaching is the best way to learn. Assign sections in the prep note so people come prepared.

0:40 – 0:55

15 min

Practice Problems or Group Discussion

Work through exam-style questions together. Discuss approaches, not just answers. This is where deep learning happens.

0:55 – 1:00

5 min

Wrap-Up & Next Session

Action items: what each person will review before next time. Schedule the next session before anyone leaves.

Icebreaker Ideas

One-Word Check-In

Each person says one word: how they feel, or one word about the topic. Fast, inclusive, no pressure.

Time: ~1 minute

Teach It in 30 Seconds

Randomly pick one concept. Give someone 30 seconds to explain it. Rest of group rates it 1–5 for clarity.

Time: ~3 minutes

Two Truths One Lie

Study-themed: share two facts and one myth about today's topic. Group votes on the lie — great for exposing misconceptions.

Time: ~5 minutes

Handling Difficult Dynamics

If someone dominates the conversation:

Use round-robin speaking turns: "Let's hear from everyone — [Name], what's your take?"

If someone is disengaged or distracted:

Give them a specific role — timekeeper, note-taker, or question-master. Responsibility increases engagement.

If energy drops mid-session:

Call a 2-minute movement break or do a quick icebreaker. Energy is contagious — model enthusiasm yourself.

If conflict arises between students:

Refocus on the shared goal: "Let's stay focused on helping each other prepare. We can discuss this offline."

After the Session

Share a summary within 2 hours via WhatsApp — key points covered, resources mentioned, next session date.

Log attendance in the portal to earn your 50 achievement points per verified study group session.

Collect one piece of feedback — "What was most useful today?" Simple and takes 10 seconds.

Schedule the next session before everyone disperses. Momentum is everything — don't let it cool off.
