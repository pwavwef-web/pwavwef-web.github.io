Event Planning Basics

5 phases · Defining outcomes · Audience sizing · Venue selection

Every successful event — from a 6-person study circle to a 100-person campus fair — follows the same five phases. Master this framework and every event you run will feel professional and purposeful.

The 5 Event Phases

1

Concept

Define the purpose. What is this event trying to achieve? What should attendees know or be able to do afterwards? Who is it for? What's the ideal format (workshop, study session, talk, social event)?

2

Planning

Book the venue or platform, create an agenda, assign roles, prepare all materials. Build a timeline working backwards from the event date. Always start planning at least 2 weeks in advance.

3

Promotion

Apply the 3-7-1 rule: announce 3 weeks, 7 days, and 1 day before the event. Use personal invites, WhatsApp groups, flyers, and social media. Target course reps and student union officers for wider reach.

4

Execution

Run the event from your prepared agenda. Arrive 30 minutes early. Have a backup plan for every key dependency (venue issue → switch to online; equipment failure → use phone audio). Keep energy high — your energy is contagious.

5

Review

Within 24 hours: send thank-you messages, share recording/summary, collect feedback. Within 3 days: submit event report to the core team via the portal.

Choosing the Right Format

Study Circle

4–8 students, focused topic, 60–90 minutes. Best for exam prep and deep learning.

Campus Workshop

10–50 students, one featured topic or skill, 90–120 minutes. Best for raising awareness and recruiting.

Virtual Session

Any size, Google Meet or Zoom, flexible timing. Best for reaching students who can't attend in person.

Planning Exercise

Choose an event you want to run next month. Using the 5 phases framework, write a 1-paragraph plan for each phase. Share your plan with your campus coordinator for approval and feedback before you start promoting.

Promoting Your Event

3-7-1 rule · WhatsApp group strategy · Personal invites · Course reps

Great events fail with poor promotion. Apply these strategies consistently and your events will always have strong attendance.

The 3-7-1 Posting Rule

3 wks

Launch post: announce the event, share the topic, and create intrigue. Focus on why it matters.

7 days

Reminder post: add more details — what to expect, who should come, any materials to prepare.

1 day

Final push: "Tomorrow at [time]! Here's the link/location. Tag someone who should come." Creates urgency.

Personal Invitations First

Before posting in any group, send at least 10 personal WhatsApp messages to students you know. Personalised invitations are 5× more likely to result in attendance than group posts. Use the student's name and mention why this specific event is relevant to them.

Leveraging Course Reps & Student Union Officers

Course reps have instant access to entire year groups. One course rep sharing your event reaches more students than a week of individual outreach. Build relationships with course reps early — invite them to your first event personally and they become natural advocates for future events.

Promotion Plan Exercise

Write 3 actual posts for your next event — one for each stage of the 3-7-1 rule. Then write a personal invitation message for a specific student you'll invite. Share all 4 with a fellow ambassador for feedback before publishing.

Running Virtual Study Sessions

Google Meet setup · Breakout rooms · Recording · Attendance tracking

Virtual sessions remove geographic barriers and allow students who can't attend in person to participate. When run well, they can be just as effective as physical sessions — and often more efficient.

Pre-Session Setup

  • • Test your audio, video and screen share before the session — never troubleshoot live
  • • Open the virtual room 15 minutes early to welcome early arrivals and do last checks
  • • Paste the session agenda in the meeting chat immediately when you open the room
  • • Ask attendees to keep their cameras on if possible — it dramatically increases engagement

Using Breakout Rooms Effectively

For sessions with 10+ participants, breakout rooms are essential. Groups of 3–5 think and share more freely than large groups. Best practice:

  • • Assign a clear task to each breakout room before sending them in
  • • Give 5–10 minutes max — attention drops after that
  • • Bring everyone back and ask one representative from each room to share key points

Recording & Consent

Always ask for verbal consent before recording: "I'd like to record today's session so attendees can watch it back — is everyone okay with that?" Share the recording link via WhatsApp within 24 hours. Store all recordings in a shared Google Drive folder for your campus.

Tracking Virtual Attendance

Use a Google Form attendance link dropped in the chat at the start of the session. This creates a timestamped record you can use in your monthly impact report. Log verified sessions in the ambassador portal to claim your achievement points.

Technical Rehearsal

Schedule a 15-minute test call with a fellow ambassador. Practise: opening the room, sharing your screen, enabling breakout rooms, dropping a link in the chat, and ending the call. This eliminates technical anxiety on the day of your actual event.

Post-Event Follow-Up

Thank-you messages · Feedback collection · Event report · Building momentum

The work doesn't end when the event does. The 24 hours after an event are the most important for building long-term community and loyalty. Done well, your follow-up turns a one-time attendee into a recurring participant.

The 24-Hour Follow-Up

0–2 hrs

Send a group thank-you message to all attendees. Mention 2–3 highlights from the session. Share your referral link as a "thank you gift".

2–4 hrs

Share the recording link (if virtual) and any resources, slides or notes from the session.

Same day

Send a feedback form — 3 to 5 questions maximum. "What was most valuable?" "What would you improve?" "Would you attend again?"

24 hrs

Log attendance in the ambassador portal to claim your achievement points. Every verified session = +50 points.

3 days

Submit your event report to the core team using the Monthly Impact Report Template.

Building Momentum for Next Time

Before your follow-up message group goes cold, announce the next session. Attendees are most enthusiastic immediately after a good event. A simple "Our next session is in 2 weeks — who's in?" before the energy fades can lock in 50–70% of your audience for the next event.

Template Creation

Write your standard post-event follow-up message template — the one you'll use after every session. Include: thank-you, 2 session highlights, resources link placeholder, feedback form link placeholder, and next session teaser. Save it somewhere you can reuse and adapt quickly after every event.
