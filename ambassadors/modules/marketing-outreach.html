Understanding Your Audience

Identify student personas · Map hotspots · Build a target profile

Before you can promote AZ Learner effectively, you need to know exactly who you are talking to. Not all students have the same needs — a first-year student struggling with lecture notes has different priorities from a finalist preparing for professional exams.

Step 1: Create Student Personas

A student persona is a semi-fictional profile representing a key segment of your campus. Build at least 3 personas before starting your outreach. For each, define:

  • • Year of study and department
  • • Primary academic challenge (e.g. managing workload, finding past questions, GPA anxiety)
  • • Social media platforms they use most
  • • Where they spend time on campus (library, cafeteria, faculty grounds)
  • • What would motivate them to try a new tool

Step 2: Map Campus Hotspots

Identify the 5 physical locations on your campus where students gather most. These are your primary outreach zones:

Library

Students here are already in study mode — ideal for introducing AZ Learner features directly related to study.

Faculty Notice Boards

Attach flyers with QR codes near exam timetables and results notices for maximum visibility.

Cafeteria / Canteen

Casual conversations over lunch — approach naturally, not like a pitch. Lead with the free access angle.

WhatsApp Course Groups

The most scalable digital hotspot. One message in a 200-person course group reaches more students than a week of in-person tabling.

Step 3: Prioritise Your Segments

Not all audiences are worth equal effort. Use the Size × Receptiveness matrix: prioritise audiences that are both large (many students) and highly receptive (exam season, freshers' week, result release period).

Practice Exercise

Write 3 student personas for your campus. For each, list: name (fictional), year, department, top challenge, and the one AZ Learner feature that would most appeal to them. Share your personas in the ambassador WhatsApp group for peer feedback.

Social Media Strategy

Content calendar · Short-form video · Posting strategy · CTAs

Social media is your most scalable outreach channel. Done well, a single post can reach hundreds of students for zero cost. Done poorly, it gets ignored. This module gives you a system that works.

The 3-Platform Approach

Focus on Instagram, TikTok and WhatsApp. These are the dominant platforms for Ghanaian university students in 2026. Do not spread yourself thin across all platforms — master these three first.

Content Calendar (Monthly Template)

Week 1

Introduction / awareness post — "Here's the tool I've been using for my studies" (Instagram + WhatsApp)

Week 2

Feature spotlight — short-form video walkthrough of one AZ Learner feature (TikTok + Instagram Reel)

Week 3

Social proof — share a peer testimonial or your own exam success story with a referral CTA

Week 4

Event promotion or study tip — tie to exam season if possible. Always include referral link.

Posting Best Practices

  • • Post at least 3× per week during exam season, 1–2× per week otherwise
  • • Use hooks in the first 3 seconds of any video — students scroll fast
  • • Add captions to all videos — many students watch without sound
  • • Always include a clear CTA: "Sign up free via the link in my bio"
  • • Use #AZLearner, #StudySmart, and your university hashtag on every post

Pro Tip: Peak Posting Times

For Ghanaian university students: Tuesday–Thursday evenings (7–10 PM) and Sunday afternoons (3–6 PM) drive the highest engagement. Post your best content during these windows.

Campus Tabling & Flyers

Table setup · 60-second pitch · QR codes · Email collection

In-person outreach builds trust faster than any digital channel. When a student sees a peer enthusiastically explaining a tool they personally use, conversion rates are dramatically higher than a social media post.

Setting Up a Campus Table

  • • Location: Near the library entrance, the main gate, or the student union building during peak hours (9–11 AM, 12–2 PM)
  • • Table setup: Laptop with AZ Learner open, printed flyers with QR codes, a small sign with your headline ("Sign up free — takes 2 minutes")
  • • Team: Always table with at least one other ambassador — two-person teams convert 40% better than solo tabling
  • • Goal: 10 new sign-ups per 2-hour tabling session

The 60-Second Pitch

Memorise this — adapt it to your natural voice:

"Hey, are you a student here? I'm [Name], an AZ Learner ambassador. AZ Learner is a free app built specifically for students in Ghana — it has past questions, a GPA calculator, and study groups so you can find classmates to study with. It takes 2 minutes to sign up and it's completely free. Want me to show you how it works?"

QR Code Strategy

Create a QR code that links to the AZ Learner sign-up page with your referral code embedded. Print at least 20 small cards and 5 A4 flyers per tabling session. Place extra QR code stickers on:

  • • Faculty notice boards (request permission from department secretary)
  • • Inside library bathrooms (if permitted)
  • • Shared study tables in the library

Collecting Emails for Follow-Up

Keep a Google Form or paper sign-up sheet at your table. Collect: name, email, and WhatsApp number. This is your follow-up list — students who don't sign up on the spot often convert within 48 hours of a personal follow-up message.

Practice Exercise

Practise delivering your 60-second pitch in front of a mirror or record a short video of yourself doing it. Time it. If it's over 75 seconds, trim it. Share your pitch video with your campus coordinator for feedback.

Measuring Your Impact

Referral tracking · Monthly targets · Impact reporting · Analytics

What gets measured gets improved. Top ambassadors track their outreach obsessively — not just to earn points, but to understand what's working and double down on it.

Key Metrics to Track Monthly

Referral Sign-ups

Track via the ambassador portal dashboard. Target: minimum 10 new sign-ups per month. Top ambassadors achieve 25–40.

Events Hosted

Log each event in the portal. Include date, type, and attendance. Target: minimum 1 event per month.

Social Media Reach

Record your post impressions and profile visits monthly. Screenshot your Instagram/TikTok analytics and include in your monthly report.

Achievement Points

Your portal dashboard shows your running total. Use it as a real-time proxy for your overall impact and activity level.

Setting SMART Goals

At the start of each month, set SMART goals: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound. Example:

❌ Weak goal: "Get more sign-ups this month"

✅ SMART goal: "Recruit 15 new students via my referral link in February by posting 3× per week on Instagram and tabling once at the library entrance on Tuesdays"

Monthly Report Submission

Submit your monthly impact report via the portal by the last day of each month. Use the Monthly Impact Report Template in the Resource Library. Accurate reports ensure your achievement points are correctly awarded and build your ambassador reputation.
