Course 1 2 hrs 3 modules

Marketing & Outreach

Learn how to effectively promote AZ Learner on campus, reach new student audiences, and drive sign-ups through targeted social media campaigns and on-campus activations.

1 Understanding Your Audience

Identify your campus demographics, map student pain points, and craft messages that resonate with different peer groups. Learn how to segment your audience by faculty, year group, and interest to deliver the right message at the right time.

Key topics:

  • Demographic mapping: faculties, year groups, interests
  • Identifying student pain points AZ Learner solves
  • Crafting value propositions for different audiences
  • Tailoring your message for each channel (in-person vs online)
2 Social Media Strategy

Build a content calendar for Instagram, TikTok, and WhatsApp to grow AZ Learner visibility on campus. Learn best practices for each platform, how to create short-form video content, and how to measure engagement to improve future posts.

Key topics:

  • Platform-specific content formats and best times to post
  • Building a two-week content calendar template
  • Creating short-form video demos of AZ Learner features
  • Tracking reach, saves, and referral link clicks
3 On-Campus Activation

Plan and run tabling events, peer-to-peer demos, and referral drives. Learn how to set up an eye-catching booth, demo the app to sceptical students, and collect sign-ups effectively using your unique referral code.

Key topics:

  • Choosing high-traffic campus locations for activations
  • The 60-second app demo script
  • Using your referral code to track sign-ups
  • Handling objections and sceptical students
Course 2 2.5 hrs 3 modules

Leadership Development

Develop the skills to lead study groups, mentor peers, and build a positive ambassador team culture. This course equips you to be the kind of leader students and teammates genuinely want to follow.

1 Effective Study-Group Facilitation

Learn how to lead productive study groups and keep participants engaged throughout a session. Discover techniques for setting clear agendas, managing time, dealing with dominant or disengaged members, and making every participant feel valued.

Key topics:

  • Setting a clear agenda before each session
  • Timeboxing activities to maintain energy
  • Managing dominant vs. quiet participants
  • Closing a session with action items and accountability
2 Mentoring Peers

Build trust with fellow students, give constructive feedback, and help them set realistic academic goals. Explore active listening, motivational techniques, and how to identify when a peer needs extra support beyond academic guidance.

Key topics:

  • The GROW coaching model (Goal, Reality, Options, Way forward)
  • Giving feedback that motivates rather than discourages
  • Recognising signs of academic stress or burnout
  • When and how to refer peers to professional support
3 Managing Conflict

Learn to resolve disputes fairly and maintain a positive ambassador team culture. Understand the root causes of interpersonal conflict, practice de-escalation strategies, and know when to escalate issues to the AZ Learner core team.

Key topics:

  • Common sources of conflict in student teams
  • De-escalation language and neutral framing
  • Facilitating a structured resolution conversation
  • When to escalate and how to document incidents
Course 3 2 hrs 3 modules

Communication Skills

Master public speaking, written communication, and active listening to engage your campus community and represent AZ Learner with confidence and clarity in every interaction.

1 Public Speaking Fundamentals

Structure a short talk, control nerves, and make a strong first impression. Practice the PREP framework (Point, Reason, Example, Point) for impromptu and prepared speeches, and learn how body language amplifies your message.

Key topics:

  • The PREP framework for structured speaking
  • Managing nerves: breathing, pausing, grounding techniques
  • Eye contact, posture, and gesture best practices
  • Opening and closing with impact
2 Written Communication

Craft clear emails, WhatsApp messages, and social-media captions that get results. Learn the principles of plain language, how to write a compelling call to action, and common mistakes that make professional communication less effective.

Key topics:

  • Plain language principles: short sentences, active voice
  • Writing subject lines and opening lines that get opened
  • Crafting a clear call to action in every message
  • WhatsApp broadcast etiquette and group message tone
3 Active Listening

Understand student feedback deeply and respond with empathy. Active listening is more than hearing words â€” it is about understanding intent, asking clarifying questions, summarising what you heard, and validating the speaker's perspective before responding.

Key topics:

  • The four levels of listening
  • Asking open-ended clarifying questions
  • Paraphrasing and summarising to confirm understanding
  • Responding with empathy without dismissing concerns
