Why We're Doing This

Students shouldn't be in the dark about their own grades

Most university students only find out their GPA when official results are released — often weeks after exams. By then, it's too late to course-correct.

Others rely on mental math or scattered spreadsheets that are error-prone and don't give them a complete picture. The frustration of being "surprised" by a lower GPA than expected is real — and preventable.

We're building the tool that should have existed years ago. But first, we need to deeply understand your experience. That's what this survey is about.
